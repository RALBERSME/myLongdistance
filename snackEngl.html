• Pocky: thin sticks of puff pastry/biscuit, coated with chocolate (often also matcha, strawberry, almond). Taste: sweet and creamy, crispy.
  • Onigiri (rice ball): rice ball with filling such as salmon, umeboshi or tuna/mayo, often wrapped in nori seaweed. Taste: salty-umami, mild.
  • Senbei: crispy rice cracker slices, sweet or salty, often soy-based. Taste: savory-salty, nutty.
  • Taiyaki: waffle with filling (typically anko) in the shape of a fish. Taste: sweet, creamy, slightly caramelized on the outside.
  • Mochi: sticky rice cake, often with a sweet filling (anko, strawberry). Taste: soft, sweet, rice-like.
  • Dorayaki: two pancakes with anko filling. Taste: sweet, slightly caramelized, mochi-like texture.
  • Manju: steamed dough hearts with filling (often anko). Taste: sweet, soft, doughy.
  • Senbei/Okaki with soy sauce or seasoning: variation from lightly salty to spicy, crispy.
  • Katsu sando-like snacks: small bread sandwiches with tonkatsu filling, rather savory.
  • Yakitori snacks: grilled chicken skewers in smaller portion sizes; often served as a snack, salty-savory.
  • Edamame: young soybeans, lightly salted. Taste: fresh, nutty, salty.
  • Takoyaki: small dough balls filled with octopus, crispy on the outside, soft on the inside; topped with mayonnaise and bonito flakes. Flavor: savory, creamy, umami.
  • Arare/Kaki no Tane: crispy rice balls/snacks with chili; spicy, slightly hot.
  • Cup Noodles/Instant Ramen Snacks: noodle snacks in cups, creamy-salty, savory.
