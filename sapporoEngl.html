Sapporo


is Hokkaido's northern metropolis. Anyone who comes here immediately feels the sense of spaciousness: wide boulevards, lush green spaces in summer, and roofs thickly covered with snow in winter.
The city originated from a seaport settlement. The region's transformation from a rural, cultivated area to a modern metropolis is reflected in its architecture, layout, and culture. The Ainu culture and its traces in the region are an important cultural context, which is also visible in museums and exhibitions.
The Sapporo-Odori route reflects the city plan of the early settlement years. There are wide streets and an open area that later became the venue for large-scale events.
Odori Park becomes the stage for the Snow Festival in February. Huge snow sculptures are erected there, which are illuminated at night with colored lights.
